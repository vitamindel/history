1843: The Aneroid Barometer

An Innovation in Pressure Measurement

In 1843, Lucien Vidi, a French scientist, invented the aneroid barometer, an alternative to the traditional mercury barometer. The term "aneroid" means "without liquid," as this barometer does not use mercury or other fluids to measure atmospheric pressure.

The aneroid barometer uses a sealed, flexible metal chamber called an aneroid cell. As atmospheric pressure changes, the chamber contracts or expands, moving a mechanical needle on a calibrated scale. This made barometers more portable and safer compared to their mercury predecessors.
